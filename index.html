The 7 Best Books On Property Investment

For a relatively small outlay, the best books on property investment will help those who are wanting to invest in property, as well as those that already have done so, learn something to boost profitability.

Not every book will meet the reader’s ambition. However, most property investment books have been written by experts who are writing about their own experiences and they offer something to learn. These books will help you:

  • Gain invaluable insights;
  • Learn practical lessons.

So, whether you are new to investing in property or are looking to learn from others’ previous experiences, or whether you already have an established property portfolio and want to keep abreast of other investors’ thoughts and tactics, these books are worth considering.

Great property investment books to read

The team at Property Road has done the research (and a lot of reading!) to compile this helpful list of great property investment books to read.

#1: The Complete Guide to Property Investment: How to survive & thrive in the new world of buy-to-let

The Complete Guide to Property Investment offers readers and investors an action plan to navigate the UK’s property landscape and come out as a winner. Using real-life examples and strategies to deliver the ways and means of achieving different investment goals. Readers will:

  • Learn how to formulate an investment strategy;
  • Understand everything about financing a property investment;
  • Learn what they should invest in;
  • How to manage an investment;
  • Understand the property cycle and use it advantageously;
  • Build a long-term profitable portfolio.

The author Rob Dix has penned several property book bestsellers and his output is aimed at helping British property investors make a success of their buy to let investment. In this book, he tackles the changing tax landscape and rising property prices and says that investors, now more than ever, need to get smarter, so they can succeed.

He adds that the investors who are underprepared and unwary well end up being squeezed from the property sector to leave strategic and educated investors to enjoy the best investment years yet.

#2: How To Be A Landlord: The Definitive Guide to Letting and Managing Your Rental Property

It’s called the definitive guide to letting and managing a rental property and the author Robert Dix makes clear that the book only covers the letting and managing of a property that the investor already owns.

How to be a Landlord highlights the various pitfalls that many landlords fall into such as not providing documentation to a tenant that may mean the landlord cannot evict them as a consequence. He also explains how landlords should deal with tenant complaints, mysterious leaks, and broken boilers. Readers will learn:

  • Everything you need to know about letting and managing a property;
  • How to find perfect tenants;
  • The essential checks to avoid nightmare tenants:
  • Setting yourself up for property success;
  • Dealing with common maintenance issues and repairs;
  • Tips from experienced landlords on how to keep tenants happy.

This latest version of How to be a Landlord has been fully updated for 2020. There are contributions from more than 50 experienced landlords to offer confidence on how to run a property investment successfully.

#3: The Book on Rental Property Investing: How to Create Wealth and Passive Income Through Intelligent Buy & Hold Real Estate Investing!

Written by Brandon R Turner, this is an American offering but it does offer some excellent strategies to help investors succeed with property investment.

The author also runs a very popular real estate investment podcast, the ‘BiggerPockets Podcast’, and he’s built up years of experience. There are 400 pages of in-depth strategies and advice to help build a valuable property portfolio. The book will:

  • Establish how investors build an achievable plan of success;
  • Strategies for finding incredible property deals;
  • How to pay for rental property.

There’s a lot to recommend Rental Property Investing and for many reasons, it is considered to be a ‘must read’ for those wanting to know how they can use rental property to build wealth.

#4: Property Magic: How to Buy Property Using Other People’s Time, Money and Experience

Property Magic by Simon Zutshi is one of the bestselling property books and the latest offering is a special 10th-anniversary edition. As an experienced property investor, Simon offers some of the secrets that he uses for his ‘Property Mastermind Programme’.

Readers will learn how they can build a successful property portfolio by using other investor’s experiences. The author makes clear that the book is aimed at opening an investor’s mind so they can look afresh at some of the potential possibilities that are available in property investment. Readers will learn:

  • Building personal wealth by helping solve people’s property problems;

One of the highlights for the book is that while it focuses on UK property market investment, it can be used in every property market in the world.

The blurb on the book promises, ‘How anyone can be financially free in the next 12 months by investing in property’. That’s a bold claim, but for many people, this book is the number one choice for property investment. Readers will learn:

Over the years, the author has helped thousands of investors achieve financial freedom with property investment. He does this by making a complicated strategy become a simple philosophy.

The author has years of experience as a real estate ‘fixer and flipper’. He offers a step-by-step plan to help investors succeed in their first, or even their next, house flipping project. Readers will learn:

  • How to finance house flipping;
  • Offer strategies for creative financing;
  • Learn how to evaluate deals quickly for profitability;
  • Which types of property are worth investing in;
  • How to identify the best investment locations.

Along with offering tips and advice on finding an investment property for flipping, there is step-by-step guidance on what investors should do to create a property with added value and how a renovated property can sell quickly and at the best possible price.

What is the best investment book for beginners?

The question, ‘What is the best investment book for beginners?’ is a difficult one to answer.

That’s because investors will have a range of reasons for investing and will have differing ambitions for their portfolio.

However, reading property investment books as a beginner is a great way to avoid making costly mistakes and help create an uneventful journey to financial success.

Along with those mentioned above, it’s worth considering ‘How to be a landlord’ by Rob Dix and ‘Buy Low, Rent High’ by Samuel Leeds.

It’s also worth considering that while there is not a single book that will answer every question and deliver success, part of the research all investors should undertake is to read several books on the subject to ensure they are well-versed in terminology and how they can deliver profits.

Are property investment books worth reading?

Most certainly there are property investment books that are worth reading, but as mentioned previously, a lot depends on what your own financial ambitions are.

Whether you want to invest in a buy to let property or begin developing property or renovating an old property to rent out, there’s a book to cover every niche question you may have.

The books also explain how you can navigate planning issues with local authorities, how you find a property for developing, and also how as a landlord, you can add value to a property.

Essentially, property investment books will help fill in the gaps in your property sector knowledge because you’ll be tapping into the experience and expertise of experts who have written books to help people like you.

Who are the best property investment authors?

While there are some very good real estate book authors, when it comes to finding the best property investment writers, it’s wise to check some of the bestseller lists.

It’s also worth checking out Angela Bryant, Carl Bayley, and John Howard who write extensively on property investment.

These authors have written more than one book and have established themselves as experts in their field. Not an easy feat when you consider how many property investment books are published in the UK every year.
